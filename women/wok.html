1. Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA) - In the United Kingdom, JSA provides financial support to individuals who are actively seeking work, including women.

2. Unemployment Insurance (UI) In the United States, UI provides temporary financial assistance to eligible workers who have lost their jobs, including women.

3. National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (NREGA) - India, NREGA guarantees 100 days of wage employment in a financial year to rural households whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work, which includes many women.

4. Workfare Income Supplement (WIS)- In Singapore, WIS provides cash supplements and CPF (Central Provident Fund) contributions to low-wage workers, including women.

5. Self-Employment Program for Unemployed Women - In Ethiopia, this program provides support for unemployed women to start their own businesses.

6. Women's Enterprise for Sustainability (WES)- Canada, WES provides funding and support to help unemployed women start and grow their businesses.

7. Women Enterprise Fund (WEF)- In Kenya, this fund supports women's entrepreneurship by providing access to finance and training.

8. Women's Empowerment and Livelihoods Program (WELP) - In Bangladesh, this program supports unemployed women by providing training, credit, and market linkages for income-generating activities.

9. Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) - In India, PMEGP provides financial assistance for setting up micro-enterprises, with a focus on unemployed women.

10. Women's Business Center (WBC) - In the United States, the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) operates WBCs that provide counseling, training, and other resources to help women start and grow businesses.
