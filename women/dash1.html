1.Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao (BBBP)

No specific eligibility criteria as it's a national campaign aimed at addressing the declining child sex ratio and promoting the education of girls.

Documents Required

1. Birth Certificate of the Girl Child (issued by the hospital or a recognized government body)

2.Proof of Identity of the Parents (Aadhaar Card, Ration Card, etc.)

3.Proof of Address of the Parents (Passport, Driving license, Utility bills like water, telephone, electricity, etc)

4.Passport size photograph

2.Samriddhi Yojana

Parents or legal guardians can open an account for a girl child below 10 years of age.

The girl child should be an Indian citizen.

There are income criteria for opening the account, which may vary depending on the scheme guidelines.

Documents Required

1.Birth certificate of the girl child.

2.Photo ID of applicant's parent or legal guardian.

3.Address proof of applicant's parent or legal guardian.

4.Other KYC proofs such as PAN, Voter ID.

3.Child Education Fund (GCEF)

Typically, this scholarship scheme targets girls from economically weaker sections of society.

Specific income criteria may be defined by the implementing agency.

About the Scheme

4.Scheme of Incentive to Girls for Secondary Education (NSIGSE)

Girls from families below the poverty line or those belonging to SC/ST communities are eligible.

There may be specific criteria related to age and educational qualifications.

Documents Required

1. Scanned copy of the applicant’s Aadhaar card.

2. Domicile Certificate.

3. Income certificate of the parents or the legal guardian (issued by the respective state government or UT administration authorities).

4. Class 10th scorecard of the applicant.

5. Certificate from the school head or the principal mentioning two-year continuation after her enrolment to Class 9th.

6. Fees receipts of the current class.

7. Bank account proof of the applicant’s existing account or joint account with any one of her parents.

8. Disability certificate (if applicable).

5.Scholarship Scheme for Girl Students

Eligibility criteria may vary depending on the specific scholarship program.

Generally, these scholarships aim to encourage girls to pursue technical education, so eligibility may depend on enrollment in specific technical courses.

Documents Required

1.Aadhaar Card: A copy of the Aadhaar card is mandatory for all applicants.

2.Mark sheets: Mark sheets of the qualifying examination and the previous year of study should be submitted.

3.Income certificate: Income certificate from the competent authority to prove that the family income does not exceed INR 8 lakhs per annum.

4.Admission proof: Proof of admission in a technical institution in the current academic year.

5.Bank details: Bank account details of the applicant's account in a nationalized bank or a public sector bank.

6.National Programme for Education of Girls at Elementary Level (NPEGEL)

This program generally targets areas with low female literacy rates.

Girls from disadvantaged backgrounds are usually prioritized.

Target Population / Beneficiaries:-

1.Out of School Girls.

2.Drop out girls.

3.Overage girls, who have not completed elementary education.

4.Working girls.

5.Girls from marginalized social groups.

6.Girls with low attendance.

7.Girls with low levels of achievement.

7.SABLA (Rajiv Gandhi Scheme for Empowerment of Adolescent Girls)

Out-of-school adolescent girls between 11 to 14 years old are eligible.

There may be specific criteria related to income and social background.

8.Shakti Yojana (KSY)

Adolescent girls in the age group of 11 to 18 years are typically eligible.

There may be criteria related to health and nutrition status.

9.Income Support Program (BISP)

Women from low-income households are eligible.

Specific criteria regarding income and family situation may apply.

10.Education Loan Scheme (TELS)

Eligibility criteria for education loans generally involve the applicant's creditworthiness and ability to repay the loan.

There may not be specific gender-related eligibility criteria, but female students are encouraged to apply.

11.WEP-ICT for Girls

Eligibility criteria may vary depending on the specific initiatives under the Women’s Entrepreneurship Program in Kenya.

Typically, girls interested in STEM education are targeted.

12.Child Network (GCN)

Girls from Zimbabwe who are in need of educational support and empowerment may be eligible.

Specific eligibility criteria may vary depending on the programs offered by GCN.

13.Program for Afghan Women

Eligibility criteria may vary depending on the specific higher education opportunities provided.

Generally, Afghan women seeking higher education are eligible.

14.Pudhumai Pen Thittam

To support the education of girl children. The scheme aims to empower girls through education and promote gender equality.
